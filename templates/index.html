ABOUT
Ah, I see you want more information about me. Well, here it is. Please don't use it nefariously.

I am a programmer and math tutor living in Vancouver, BC. I studied at Quest University Canada in Squamish, where I received a Bachelor's of Arts and Sciences with a focus in mathematics. At Quest, students center their education on a question of their choosing; I asked, "How can I make simple things complicated?"

We are surrounded by simple things. Bread seems pretty simple. Lighters are simple. The patterns in linoleum or wallpaper are simple. I like to see what kind of complexity can be derived from the simple things. What makes the air bubbles in bread? Why does the flame on a lighter initially seem to jump up when lit? How are the patterns of wallpaper fundamentally similar or different?

I find there's a lot to be learned from exploring the hidden intricacies of things.

You can find my additional projects on GitHub at https://github.com/ahblay and Kaggle at https://www.kaggle.com/ahblay.

Oh, and check out this report on me in the world-famous Sonoma Index Tribune: https://www.sonomanews.com/news/3387774-181/local-student-studies-in-antarctica .
